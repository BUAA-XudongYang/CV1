PROJECTS

  • 2017.01-2017.07

    Finite element simulation: Impact damage prediction of assembly fiber-reinforced thermoplastic polymer structures

    Description: Assembly fiber-reinforced thermoplastic polymer (AFRTP) is a hybrid material manufactured by injection of short/long fiber-reinforced thermoplastic resin on the back of continuous fiber-reinforced laminate performed by thermal stamping. Using the finite element method, the low-speed impact is simulated and the damage mechanisms of the interface is revealed. One EI paper was published (First author).

  • 2017.09-2018.02

    Structure Design: Integrated design of a SUV composite tailgate.

    Description: Under the background of automobile lightweighting, an integrated “material-structure-process-performance” method is proposed for the conceptual and detail design of composite components. Using the concept of “plastic-generation-steel” and the finite element method, the proposed method was validated by an application case of automotive tailgate using carbon fiber reinforced polymer. One SCI paper was published and the program was referred by some enterprises (First author).

  • 2018.02-2018.05

    Material Preparation and Characterization: Preparation and characterization of CNT/CB hybrid nanocomposites for structural health monitoring.

    Description: Under the trend of automotive intelligence, through the preparation of new CNT/CB nanocomposites, I explore its application as a smart material in the health monitoring of structures. One SCI paper was submitted (First author).

  • 2018.03-Now

    Research Innovation Program for Postgraduates of BUAA: Modulating the sensing behaviors of styrene-ethylene-butylene-styrene/carbon nanotubes with carbon black for sensor applications.

    Description: As the leader of our application group, I help to win the support from the Research Innovation Program for Postgraduates of BUAA for our project ‘Modulating the sensing behaviors of styrene-ethylene-butylene-styrene/carbon nanotubes with carbon black for sensor applications’ (Advanced Aerospace Vehicle Innovation Research Base of Beihang University, No. YCSJ-01-2018-13).

  • 2018.05-2018.12

    Material Preparation and Characterization: Preparation and characterization of CNT/CB hybrid nanocomposites for large deformation sensor applications.

    Description: Inspired by the technology of using carbon nanotubes to mix with polymers for smart health monitoring, this work explores a promising tunable technology for large deformation sensors through the incorporation of conductive carbon allotropes into thermoplastic elastomers. One SCI paper was published and a presentation was made at the international conference (First author).

  • 2018.09-Now

    Material Preparation and Characterization: Preparation and characterization of CNT/CB hybrid nanocomposites for large deformation sensor applications.

    Description: Continuing the previous work, this work aims to explore the effects of the temperatures on the properties (mechanical properties, piezoresistive properties, etc.) of nanocomposites for large deformation sensors.
